Infinite Scroll

Content loads automatically as you scroll down. No "next page" button. Just keep scrolling to see older content.

Pros

  • Fluid, modern feel
  • Great for discovery serendipity
  • No clicks to load more

Cons

  • Hard to jump to specific dates
  • Scroll position gets lost easily
  • Can't reach footer/links easily
ShadowBrain

Pagination

Content split into pages (20 items per page). Clear "next/prev" controls. Each page is a discrete unit.

Pros

  • Predictable, stable URLs
  • Easy to jump around
  • Performance is consistent
  • Can share specific pages

Cons

  • Requires clicks to see more
  • Feels dated for feed-style content
  • Harder to scan chronologically
ShadowBrain

"Load More" Button

Hybrid approach. Shows a chunk of content, then a button at bottom to load the next batch. Scroll position is stable within each batch.

Pros

  • Best of both worlds
  • User controls when to load
  • Can stop anytime (unlike infinite)
  • Still feels modern

Cons

  • Still requires a click
  • Hard to reach very old content
  • Button can be easy to miss
